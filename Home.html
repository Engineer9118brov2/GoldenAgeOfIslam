Medicine

Revolutionary medical practices and hospitals

Ibn Sina's Canon of Medicine: The Medical Encyclopedia

"The Canon of Medicine" was a book where all the world’s medical knowledge could be found in one place. For hundreds of years, the book "The Canon of Medicine," was the go-to place to get information about medicine. The book was massive five-volume encyclopedia written by Ibn Sina around the year 1025. Ibn Sina was a brilliant Persian doctor and philosopher, who gathered medical ideas from around ancient Greece, Rome, India, and his own Islamic culture. His job was not to just copy knowledge from around the world: He organized it, tested it, and added his own discoveries.

The Canon was detailed and well-written, which made it one of the best medical textbook for over 600 years in both the Islamic world and Europe. It described over 800 different drugs and was one of the first books to suggest that diseases could be spread through contaminated water and soil. It talked about everything from how the body works to complex surgery.

A Book That Changed the World

"The Canon of Medicine" was a revolutionary book. It introduced the idea of quarantine to prevent the spread of contagious diseases—a strategy we still use today to stop illnesses from spreading. Ibn Sina also created one of the first strategies that test drugs. Without his work, doctors might not have understood the importance of hygiene, diet, and testing medicines and drugs safely. Life would be very different, as we would lack some of the basic medical rules that keep us healthy and help us recover from illnesses.

Mathematics

Foundations of algebra and trigonometry

Al-Khwarizmi: The Father of Algebra

Algebra is the a branch of mathematics in which letters and other symbols are used to represent numbers and quantaties in formulas and equations. Algebra is a tool developed by the Persian mathematician Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi in the 9th century. Working in Baghdad's famous House of Wisdom, he didn't invent Algebra: He studied and improved upon the ideas of ancient Greek and Indian scholars.

Al-Khwarizmi wrote a book that provided a clear guide for solving equations, creating the system of algebra we still learn today. He also supported the use of the Hindu-Arabic numeral system: The same numbers 0 through 9 we use today. The numeral system made calculations much simpler than before.

Math That Powers Our World

Al-Khwarizmi's work was so influential that his name gave us the word "algorithm"—the set of instructions that powers computer programs. Every time you use GPS to find a location, watch a movie with CGI, or play a video game, you are seeing algebra in action. Without his achievements, the technology we rely on today would not exist: From online shopping to space technology. Many people cannot imagine a day without your phone or computer: That's a world without algebra.

Literature

The Thousand and One Nights

The Thousand and One Nights: A Story Within a Story

"The Thousand and One Nights," also known as "The Arabian Nights," is a collection of stories written during the Islamic Golden Age. The book tells the story of a queen named Scheherazade. Her husband, the king, is cruel and plans to kill her. To save her life, she tells him a new story every night, but she always leaves the story on a cliffhanger, so the king has to keep her alive to hear the ending.

The stories in the book come from all over the Middle East, Persia, and India. The stories were collected and translated into Arabic over hundreds of years and were taught and read to children across Islam. The most famous stories, like "Aladdin and the Wonderful Lamp" and "Ali Baba and the Forty Thieves," were actually added to the book much later by European translators.

Stories That Traveled the World

The influence of "The Thousand and One Nights" is widespread. It has been translated into almost every language and continues to inspire new stories. For example, the famous Disney movie "Aladdin" is based on one of its tales, and the adventures of "Sinbad the Sailor" have been turned into multiple films. These stories of magic, love, and adventure have become a part of global culture. The stories are a reminder of how a collection of stories from the Islamic Golden Age can continue to capture the imagination of people all over the world, even thousands of years later.
